B: Data Type
- Population (Thousands) is quantitative.
- City (Edinburgh, Stockholm, Florence, etc.) is nominal.

C: Visual Channels
- Population (Thousands) is varying in horizontal position on an unaligned scale.
- City (Edinburgh, Stockholm, Florence, etc.) is varying in vertical position on an aligned scale.

D: Scale Break
The scale break is effective because since there is a large gap in the data points - with Naples, Paris, Constantinople, and London having significantly higher values than the other cities, the scale break allows the data points for these 4 cities to still be kept within the chart without distorting the appearance or spacing in the other data points.

F: Visual Channels
- Domain area (Biotech, ICT, Humanities, etc.) is varying in color hue.
- Social impact is varying in vertical position on an aligned scale.
- Capital investment is varying in volume.

G: 3D Bubble Chart Visualization

This visualization can be ineffective because the numerous axes and dimensions can make it more difficult to determine the coordinates of a specific data point and thus negatively impact the accuracy of the data. For instance, it is hard to tell what the exact radii are for the domains ranked 5 through 9 due to their volumes appearing to be similar, as well as what the exact height of the domain ranked 2 since it does not appear to "hover in space" the way the other domains are.

#4

A: The type of visualization is parallel coordinates.

B: Each of the horizontal red and blue lines represents a data entry (in this case, a car in the database) plotted against 7 attributes which are represented by the 7 axes.

C: Advantage & Drawback
- Advantage: Since each data entry is represented by a line connecting the axes, it is easy to understand the relationship between the variables (axes) by observing the pattern/trend in the collection of lines.
- Drawback: If there are a lot of data points, especially ones sharing common values, the visual can become over-cluttered with overlaying lines, making it illegible and difficult to distinguish the lines (each entry) from each other.
